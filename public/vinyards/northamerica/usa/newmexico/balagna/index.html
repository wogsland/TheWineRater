Balagna Winery

Los Alamos, New Mexico

John Balagna was a nuclear chemist at Los Alamos National Lab and worked on the Manhattan Project during World War II. In retirement he has taken up winemaking and has a tasting room next to his house on 223 Rio Bravo Drive. Tasting is free, but unfortunately Balagna's insistance on using CO2 to preserve the tasting room wine from oxidation (leading it to all have an acidic taste) and the swarms of fruit flies divebombing your tasting glass make it hard to get a good taste of anything.
